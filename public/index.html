Biography

Philip is an Economics graduate with some experience in applied microeconometrics and machine learning. Previously a data science instructor at The Center of Applied Data Science, he believes in the power of new data sources to derive critical insights to inform the direction of public policy. He is interested in topics in labour, health and development economics, and occassionally contributes to open-source projects such as the conda-forge software distribution project. He is also package maintainer of the R package bnmr and an author of pmdplyr.
